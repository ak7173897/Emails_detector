    What is EmailShield AI?

    EmailShield AI is an email safety assistant. You can paste any suspicious email and the system will label it as Safe, Spam, AI Phishing, or Uncertain.

    Phishing vs Spam (Important Difference)

    • Spam Email: Unwanted promotional or bulk messages. Usually annoying, sometimes risky, but often sent for marketing.
    • Phishing Email: A fake message designed to steal your personal data like passwords, OTPs, card details, or login access.
    • Simple rule: Spam wastes your time. Phishing can steal your identity, money, or account.

    How It Works (Simple Steps)

    1 You add the email Paste the email text or upload a file. Include as much text as possible (subject + body) for better results.
    2 The system checks warning signs It looks for signs like urgent pressure, fake account alerts, suspicious links, and requests for private information.
    3 AI compares with known patterns Your email is compared with patterns learned from many real examples of safe, spam, and phishing messages.
    4 You see the result The detector shows one label: Safe, Spam, AI Phishing, or Uncertain, along with a confidence score.
    5 You take safe action If it is phishing or uncertain, do not click links. Open the official website directly and verify there.

    How Spam Looks in Real Life

    Spam messages often push offers like "Win now!", "Limited time deal", or repeated promotions you never asked for. They may contain too many links, too many exclamation marks, and generic greetings.

    How Phishing Looks in Real Life

    Phishing emails pretend to be from banks, payment apps, delivery services, or your company. They try to scare you ("account blocked") or rush you ("verify in 10 minutes") so you share private information.

    Uncertainty Detection

    When the model confidence is below 70%, it returns "Uncertain" instead of making a potentially wrong prediction. This helps reduce false positives and encourages manual review of edge cases.

    How to Read the Confidence Score

    90-100% = strong match. 70-89% = likely, but double-check important emails. Below 70% = uncertain, so verify manually before you trust the email.

    Future Scope

    The proposed system can be further enhanced by integrating it directly with email platforms such as Gmail, enabling automated scanning of incoming emails without requiring manual copy-paste by the user. This can be achieved through secure API integration, allowing the system to access and analyze emails in real time. Such an extension would improve usability, provide continuous monitoring, and deliver instant classification results for spam and phishing threats, making the system more efficient and practical for real-world deployment.

    Privacy and Safety Notes

    This detector is a decision support tool, not a replacement for security policy. Always avoid sharing passwords, OTPs, or bank details by email. For suspicious account notices, open official websites directly instead of clicking email links.
